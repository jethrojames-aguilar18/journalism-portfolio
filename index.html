When Rainy Season Begins

By: Jethro James S. Aguilar | Published on August 11, 2018


Rainy Season has set foot!

Over the past days, ceaseless heavy rains continue to pound Luzon which may lead to an increase of diseases related to contaminated water, floods and mosquitos which greatly affects the Filipinos, particularly in Metro Manila.

Here are five rainy day diseases that the public should watch out for this rainy season, as listed by the Department of Health (DOH).

1. Typhoid Fever - It is a type of enteric fever, along with paratyphoid fever. The cause is the bacterium Salmonella Typhi, also known as Salmonella enterica serotype Typhi, growing in the intestines and blood.Typhoid is spread by eating or drinking food or water contaminated with the feces of an infected person.

2. Cholera - Cholera is an infectious disease that causes severe watery diarrhea, which can lead to dehydration and even death if untreated. It is caused by eating food or drinking water contaminated with a bacterium called Vibrio cholerae.

3. Leptospirosis - Leptospirosis is a bacterial disease that affects humans and animals. It is caused by bacteria of the genus Leptospira. In humans, it can cause a wide range of symptoms, some of which may be mistaken for other diseases. Some infected persons, however, may have no symptoms at all.

4. Influenza - Influenza, commonly known as "the flu", is an infectious disease caused by an influenza virus. Symptoms can be mild to severe. The most common symptoms include: high fever, runny nose, sore throat, muscle pains, headache, coughing, sneezing, and feeling tired.

5. Dengue - Dengue fever is a mosquito-borne tropical disease caused by the dengue virus. Symptoms typically begin three to fourteen days after infection. This may include a high fever, headache, vomiting, muscle and joint pains, and a characteristic skin rash.

Prevention is better than cure! Have yourself a good lifestyle so that you would not feel any symptoms given above. If so, consult your physician immediately to help you cure the symptoms to halt the sickness to grow and adapt to your body. Doing so, you can enjoy your day free from sickness.

Polio outbreak was recently declared in the Philippines by the World Health Organization (WHO) and the Department of Health (DOH) last September 19, 2019. It returns to the Philippines after a 3 year old was reportedly diagnosed in Lanao Del Sur and this is the first case after WHO was declared polio-free last 2000.

Poliomyelitis commonly known as polio is a highly infectious disease caused by poliovirus that can lead into a lifelong paralysis or even death. Children in the country are at risk because of the debilitating disease.

Polio Outbreak is not a joke. It is incurable as said by the DOH. However, like other problems, a solution is always there. And the solution to Polio is none other than VACCINE!

DOH and partners are working together to respond to the comprehensive outbreak. One of the actions that they did was conducting a campaign called "Sabayang Patak Kontra Polio" wherein they reported 96% coverage of those who were being vaccinated against polio.

The Vaccination focuses on children below 5 years old to keep them protected as they grow up and be nurtured.

The Global Polio Eradication Initiative (GPEI), a public-private partnership is supporting the campaign that the Philippine Government conducted. GPEI has its goal and that is to suppress the cases of polio nationwide.

According to DOH, the vaccination campaign will expand in Metro Manila targeting 1,276, 658 under 5 year old and commenced in Davao and Lanao Del Sur last October this year. The campaign will spread around our country so that the cases will be eradicated.

The question is, if the vaccine is only to be applied to the children, then how about the old? The answer: they won't be getting any vaccines.

Why? Because the old people as proven by the DOH says that they have the lowest chance of having Polio. Lucky them!

To briefly summarize, let us uphold the quote, 'Prevention is better than Cure'. Before the situation worsens, let us prevent polio through Vaccination because there's no other cure than doing that.


'Mangosteen' to ease Microscopic Problems

By: Jethro James S. Aguilar | Published on November 29, 2019

However, not all school's are gifted with a very functional microscope but for a 15-year-old Junior High School Student like me to provide an alternative solution, it is undeniably brilliant!

Upon my journey as a Grade 10, several problems befell on my life, and the most memorable was my Grade 7 days.

However, the height of my expectations lowered for the reason that the microscopes that we're about to use show very unclear images. Therefore, how can we study that lesson if the medium for learning is very unfunctional?

This is when my research teacher Sir Alcayde that why don't I do a research about that by staining the specimens that were about to be lessons?'' And so I did it, but only when I am in Grade 10 so that I have access to our school's laboratory, in the point that I can reserve it for my own.

Mr. Alcayde told me to gather literature about staining and I found Mangosteen as a potential staining agent to help solve microscopic problems.

The specimens that I stained are two; a Plant Species and an Animal Species. I prepared it through the help of my research advisers and co-researchers.

Firstly, I removed the peels of the mangosteen and the rinds that I used to make 2 varying extracts, ethanolic and aqueous extracts. In the ethanolic extract, I subjected the macerated rinds to the 95% ethanol while I used 20 ml of distilled water for the aqueous extract. After that I prepared the plant and animal species which are Onion (Allium cepa) and Wild-type Fruit Fly (Drosophila Melanogaster).

For the staining part, I first stained the Onion. I got the cells from the onion, following the study of Basitah and for the next part is the fruit fly which I got out the larvae and the larva’s salivary glands for staining.

Jumping to the results, both of them were stained by the Mangosteen Rind Extract. Truly beneficial and helpful for the next generation.

Pimples, no more

By: Jethro James S. Aguilar | Published on August 4, 2018


Afraid of making friends because of pimples? Looking for ways to eradicate it completely? Trying hard to conceal it by the use of concealers? Is that you? A teenager who experiences pimple outbreaks.

Pimples usually develop after blackheads or whiteheads become inflamed and infected. This happens when the skin tries to adjust to hormonal changes that typically occur during puberty. The question is: Are you desperate to get rid of your pimples? If so, here are some ways to get rid of pimples.

First, cleanse your skin twice daily. Pimples and blackheads are more likely to appear if your skin is dirty. Use a facial soap or appropriate cleanser to remove makeup, excess oil, debris and bacteria from your skin.

Second, use witch hazel on your skin. Witch hazel acts as a natural substitute for toner or astringent. It helps to balance skin's oil levels and dry out pimples and blackheads. Use daily to promote clear skin.

Third, steam your skin. By steaming your skin once to twice weekly, you'll experience fewer blackheads and pimples.

Fourth, create a paste by mixing equal parts baking soda and water. Apply the paste to your blackheads and pimples. Leave it on the affected skin for approximately 20 minutes before rinsing. The baking soda absorbs excess oil and kills bacteria, helping to get rid of your blackheads and pimples.

Fifth, Rub lemon juice on your blackheads and pimples. The natural acidity of lemon juice dries out skin, absorbing oil that causes blackheads and pimples. It also promotes a more even complexion, helping your skin to look smoother and healthier.

Lastly, exfoliate your skin. Create a scrub by mixing equal parts granulated sugar and water. Apply the scrub to your skin, using circular motions to exfoliate the area.

Do this everyday with the right prescriptions. Then you will live and enjoy your life with no hiding and using of concealers, and more importantly, free with pimples!

PH, officially smoke free

By: Jethro James S. Aguilar | Published on May 20, 2017


The Executive Order NO. 26 entitled Providing for the Establishment of a Smoke Free Environment in Public and Enclosed Places, was issued by the Philippine President Rodrigo Roa Duterte last May 16, 2017. Due to this, many people gave positive feedback while some did the opposite.

This law was established in order to minimize the access to tobacco products, in order to provide a more supportive environment for those who are attempting to quit tobacco use which definitely pleased a lot of consumers.

This law is also beneficial for Filipinos who are fed up with inhaling second-hand smoke.Second-hand smoking is an indirect smoking that the person is inhaling the smoke the other person breathes out using the tobacco. In public places, these are most commonly experienced because many people just randomly smoke everywhere which is displeasing to others as they have no choice but to inhale it as well. Therefore, this law is very useful in such circumstances.

On the dark side, few Filipinos who lived their life being used to smoking have to give up for the sake of their lives. They were used to smoking in public and private places which is now a restricted area. If they do it, they will get arrested, which displeases the majority of smokers.

Having this law passed does not mean smoking is banned. It can still be used but not in restricted areas such as public places and workplaces.

Overall, Duterte has taken one step closer to make our country a more desirable and a more organized one. A country with no harm, drug and crime free and especially smoke free.
